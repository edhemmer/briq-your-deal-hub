# BRIX Product Constitution and Engineering Standard

## Authority

This document is the governing product constitution and highest product authority for BRIX.

Every product decision, workflow, calculation, data model, interface, AI behavior, report, integration, test, web implementation, native iOS implementation, backend service, and administrative control must conform to this document.

If implementation behavior conflicts with this constitution, the constitution takes precedence until it is intentionally amended.

Codex, developers, designers, testers, product managers, and future contributors must read this document before making material changes.

# Section 1 — Executive Vision, Mission, and Product Identity

## 1.1 Purpose

BRIX is a Property Deal Relationship Management platform, or PDRM, and a production-grade real estate investment operating system.

It exists to help investors move from opportunity to confident action with less uncertainty, less wasted time, stronger evidence, and more defensible decisions.

BRIX manages the complete relationship between an investor and a real estate opportunity from first discovery through screening, underwriting, research, visit, negotiation, offer, contract, due diligence, financing, closing, ownership, improvement, operation, refinancing, disposition, and exit.

BRIX is not designed to replace the investor. It is designed to make the investor better.

## 1.2 Vision

BRIX will become the most trusted real estate investment decision platform by combining deterministic financial analysis, evidence-based intelligence, professional workflow management, contextual education, and explainable AI inside one canonical deal record.

The platform should serve both:

- a beginner evaluating a first investment property
- an experienced investor with decades of residential, multifamily, commercial, land, or development experience

Both users receive the same professional-grade calculations, strategy logic, and evidence standards. The difference is presentation, guidance, and depth, not the quality of the underlying analysis.

## 1.3 Mission

Real estate investing should not require disconnected spreadsheets, calculators, websites, documents, notebooks, emails, maps, photos, and memory simply to determine whether a property deserves more time.

BRIX unifies those activities into one coherent operating system.

The mission is to:

1. Reduce uncertainty.
2. Improve underwriting quality.
3. Surface material risks earlier.
4. Compare every compatible investment strategy.
5. Explain recommendations clearly.
6. Preserve every material fact, assumption, document, decision, and change.
7. Help investors collaborate more effectively with realtors, attorneys, lenders, inspectors, appraisers, contractors, insurance professionals, property managers, partners, and other specialists.
8. Reduce unnecessary travel, repeated work, missed deadlines, and avoidable mistakes.
9. Help newer investors grow into confident, capable, and informed investors.
10. Support professional investors without slowing them down or hiding advanced detail.

## 1.4 Product Identity

BRIX is not:

- a listing website
- a generic CRM
- a mortgage calculator
- a spreadsheet replacement
- an AI chatbot
- a document repository
- a note-taking app
- a property management platform
- a disconnected collection of modules

BRIX is a PDRM operating system.

The property is the asset.

The deal is the lifecycle.

The evidence is the truth.

The underwriting is the financial foundation.

The recommendation is the current conclusion.

The investor remains the decision maker.

Everything revolves around one canonical deal.

## 1.5 Central Product Question

Every deal workspace must answer:

> What is the current state of this opportunity, what is the strongest available strategy, what evidence supports that conclusion, what risks remain, and what should the investor do next?

Every capability must help answer that question faster, more accurately, or more defensibly.

## 1.6 The Investor Promise

Every investor who opens BRIX should be able to understand:

- what is known
- what is estimated
- what is assumed
- what is inferred
- what is missing
- what is conflicting
- what requires verification
- which strategies are compatible
- which strategy currently ranks highest
- how much capital may be required
- what risks could change the decision
- what next action is recommended
- why that action is recommended

BRIX must reduce uncertainty without creating false certainty.

## 1.7 Core Product Outcome

BRIX must help an investor move from opportunity to decision with the fewest practical steps.

The user should be able to:

1. Enter a property address, paste a listing URL, or create a manual opportunity.
2. Select an intended strategy as a starting hypothesis.
3. Gather or estimate property, county, market, rent, financing, tax, insurance, zoning, governance, and area information.
4. Make visible and editable assumptions when complete data is unavailable.
5. Run the deterministic engine for the intended strategy.
6. Run every other compatible strategy engine for that property and jurisdiction.
7. Compare financial outcomes, risks, capital requirements, execution complexity, and confidence.
8. Receive a clear recommendation and next action.
9. Decide whether the property is worth research, a call, a visit, negotiation, an offer, monitoring, or a pass.
10. Add photos, voice notes, emails, contracts, inspections, appraisals, financing terms, quotes, estimates, governance documents, and field observations.
11. Re-underwrite the same canonical deal whenever new evidence arrives.
12. Preserve what changed, why it changed, and how the recommendation evolved.
13. Create reports, spreadsheets, property comparisons, visit lists, offer packages, and decision summaries.
14. Return later without losing work, assumptions, evidence, relationships, or decision history.

# Section 2 — Product Philosophy, Constitutional Principles, and Engineering Laws

## 2.1 Product Philosophy

Real estate investing is the practice of making consequential decisions under uncertainty.

Every property begins with incomplete information. Listing data may be wrong or incomplete. Public records may be stale. Market rents may be uncertain. Taxes may reset after sale. Insurance may materially alter operating costs. Photographs may hide defects. Contracts may contain unfavorable terms. Financing may introduce conditions that change the economics. Inspections, appraisals, governance documents, and field observations may materially change the opportunity.

BRIX exists to transform that uncertainty into informed action.

The platform continuously collects, classifies, preserves, analyzes, and explains the evidence associated with a property while providing deterministic underwriting, strategy comparison, professional workflow support, contextual education, and complete decision transparency.

BRIX never replaces the investor.

BRIX strengthens the investor.

Complexity belongs inside the system.

Clarity belongs on the screen.

## 2.2 Investor First

The investor is the primary beneficiary of every BRIX capability.

The software must not optimize for data collection, feature count, visual novelty, AI output volume, or internal technical convenience at the expense of the investor's decision.

Every feature, calculation, workflow, report, notification, lesson, and recommendation must answer at least one of these questions:

- Does this improve the decision?
- Does this reduce uncertainty?
- Does this save meaningful time?
- Does this strengthen the evidence?
- Does this improve understanding?
- Does this reduce risk?
- Does this move the opportunity forward?

If the answer is no, the capability does not belong in BRIX.

## 2.3 Truth Above Convenience

BRIX must represent reality as accurately as the available evidence allows.

Convenience, speed, aesthetics, automation, conversion goals, and artificial intelligence must never compromise factual accuracy.

Every material value must be classified as one of:

- Confirmed Fact
- User-Entered Fact
- External Estimate
- System Estimate
- User Assumption
- AI Observation
- Professional Opinion
- Inferred Information
- Unknown
- Conflict

These classifications must remain visible and preserved through reports, exports, web, iOS, and future re-underwriting.

BRIX must never present an estimate as a confirmed fact, an inference as a professional opinion, or an AI observation as an inspection finding.

## 2.4 Evidence Before Opinion

Verified and source-linked evidence takes precedence over unsupported opinion.

When reliable evidence conflicts, BRIX must preserve the conflict, surface it clearly, and explain its effect on the analysis.

The system must not silently choose the most favorable value or automatically select the newest value when source quality, effective date, or context indicates otherwise.

Evidence quality depends on:

- source authority
- source date
- effective date
- jurisdiction
- property relevance
- document integrity
- extraction confidence
- user confirmation
- professional authorship
- consistency with other evidence

## 2.5 Never Guess

BRIX must not fabricate missing information.

When a required input is unavailable, the system may:

1. Use a transparent estimate.
2. Use a range.
3. Use a documented default appropriate to the geography and property type.
4. Ask the user for a value.
5. Mark the item unknown.
6. Continue with reduced confidence when responsible.
7. Stop a calculation only when proceeding would create a materially misleading result.

The system must explain which approach was used and how the missing information affects confidence.

A useful preliminary analysis is preferable to an unnecessary hard stop, but a useful estimate must never be represented as certainty.

## 2.6 One Professional Engine, Multiple Presentation Modes

BRIX uses one authoritative calculation and recommendation system for every user.

It must not maintain a beginner engine and a professional engine.

The difference is presentation.

### Guided Mode

Guided Mode should provide:

- plain-language explanations
- contextual education
- recommended defaults
- why each input matters
- common beginner mistakes
- questions to ask professionals
- clear next steps
- progressive disclosure of advanced detail

### Professional Mode

Professional Mode should provide:

- dense financial views
- complete assumptions
- source metadata
- full financing structures
- strategy-by-strategy outputs
- scenario and sensitivity controls
- formula traceability
- detailed exports
- minimal unnecessary teaching

Changing presentation mode must never change the underlying deal, formulas, evidence, recommendation, or engine result.

## 2.7 Deterministic Intelligence and Cognitive Intelligence

BRIX has two distinct forms of intelligence.

### Deterministic Intelligence

Deterministic intelligence owns:

- financial calculations
- ratios
- debt schedules
- cash-flow models
- strategy-specific formulas
- sensitivity calculations
- scenario calculations
- score inputs
- rule-based compatibility
- rule-based disqualifiers
- tax and expense treatment logic where explicitly modeled

Identical inputs must produce identical outputs.

### Cognitive Intelligence

Cognitive intelligence may:

- explain
- summarize
- compare
- organize
- identify patterns
- extract information
- interpret documents
- interpret photographs
- interpret voice notes
- generate questions
- identify potential risks
- teach concepts
- draft narrative recommendations

Cognitive intelligence may not become the authoritative source of financial calculations, legal conclusions, engineering conclusions, appraisal conclusions, inspection conclusions, or lender decisions.

## 2.8 Explainability Is Mandatory

Every material recommendation must explain:

- what BRIX recommends
- why it recommends it
- what evidence supports it
- what evidence weakens it
- what assumptions materially influence it
- what risks remain
- what information is missing
- what could change the conclusion
- why another strategy ranks higher or lower
- what the investor should do next

The explanation must be understandable by a first-time investor while remaining technically defensible to an experienced professional.

A score without explanation is insufficient.

## 2.9 Confidence and Risk Are Separate

Confidence measures how strongly the available evidence supports the analysis.

Risk measures the potential severity and probability of adverse outcomes.

A deal may have:

- high confidence and high risk
- high confidence and low risk
- low confidence and high risk
- low confidence and low risk

BRIX must never combine confidence and risk into one ambiguous number.

Confidence should be available by category, including:

- financial confidence
- property confidence
- market confidence
- rent confidence
- financing confidence
- photo confidence
- document confidence
- governance confidence
- strategy confidence
- overall decision confidence

## 2.10 Time Awareness

Every material fact, estimate, calculation, recommendation, and report must be time-aware.

BRIX should preserve:

- source date
- effective date
- retrieval date
- calculation date
- recommendation date
- expiration or stale date where applicable

Every recommendation must be understood as an as-of conclusion based on the evidence available at that time.

## 2.11 Continuous Underwriting

A deal is never treated as static.

New evidence must update the existing canonical deal rather than create a disconnected analysis.

Examples include:

- updated market data
- visit notes
- photos
- voice notes
- contractor estimates
- inspection reports
- appraisal reports
- insurance quotes
- financing quotes
- lender commitments
- contracts and amendments
- HOA, COA, POA, or other governance documents
- title, survey, zoning, or environmental information

When new evidence changes a material input, BRIX must:

1. Preserve the prior value.
2. Record the new evidence.
3. Explain the change.
4. Recalculate affected outputs.
5. Re-rank affected strategies.
6. Update the recommendation if necessary.
7. Preserve the before-and-after decision state.

## 2.12 The Investor Decides

BRIX recommends.

The investor decides.

The user may override a recommendation, assumption, classification, next action, or strategy selection when permitted.

Overrides must:

- be explicit
- identify the user
- preserve the prior value
- include an optional reason
- appear in the audit trail
- never be silently applied

## 2.13 Cost of Delay

Time spent gathering scattered information, driving to poor opportunities, repeating calculations, searching through documents, missing deadlines, or recreating lost context has a real cost.

BRIX must reduce the cost of delay by:

- screening weak opportunities earlier
- surfacing decision-changing information sooner
- reducing duplicate entry
- keeping evidence connected
- creating efficient visit routes
- preserving prior work
- identifying the next action clearly
- recalculating immediately when material evidence changes

Speed matters, but speed must never be achieved by hiding uncertainty or compromising accuracy.

## 2.14 Core Engineering Laws

These laws are permanent unless this constitution is formally amended.

### Law 1 — One Property Identity

A property must have one canonical identity even when addresses, parcel numbers, listing URLs, ownership records, or source formatting differ.

Potential duplicates must be detected and resolved without silently merging unrelated properties.

### Law 2 — One Canonical Deal

Every opportunity has one canonical Deal ID.

Every fact, assumption, document, photo, note, contact, task, visit, calculation, strategy result, offer, contract, inspection, appraisal, financing record, recommendation, and decision attaches to that deal.

### Law 3 — One Source of Financial Truth

Web, native iOS, reports, spreadsheets, exports, admin tools, and AI explanations must use the same authoritative financial outputs.

No client may maintain a competing calculation engine.

### Law 4 — AI Never Owns the Math

AI may explain calculated results but may not become the authoritative source of money, returns, ratios, debt service, offer limits, or strategy outputs.

### Law 5 — Preserve History

Material evidence, assumptions, calculations, offers, recommendations, and decisions must not be destructively overwritten.

The current state may change, but the prior state remains traceable.

### Law 6 — Every Recommendation Is Traceable

A recommendation must be reproducible from its evidence, assumptions, formulas, engine versions, and user overrides.

### Law 7 — No Feature Exists in Isolation

Every capability must connect to the canonical deal through defined data contracts.

Examples:

- voice notes create evidence and may suggest underwriting updates
- inspection findings affect repair assumptions, offer position, and strategy ranking
- appraisal results affect value, financing, and refinance feasibility
- governance restrictions affect strategy compatibility
- contract terms affect deadlines, risk, and offer alignment
- financing terms affect cash required, debt service, returns, and maximum offer

### Law 8 — Mobile Is Not Desktop Shrunk Down

The native iPhone and iPad experience must be designed for field use, one-handed interaction, camera capture, voice input, maps, limited connectivity, and quick decisions.

### Law 9 — Every Visible Control Must Work

No dead buttons, fake success states, placeholder analytics, decorative controls, simulated integrations, or incomplete workflows may appear as production-ready behavior.

### Law 10 — Fail Visibly and Recover Gracefully

When a data source, integration, upload, calculation, or AI service fails, BRIX must:

- show the failure clearly
- preserve user work
- provide a retry path
- allow manual continuation where responsible
- avoid false success messages

### Law 11 — Security and Privacy Are Product Requirements

Authentication, authorization, data isolation, storage, deletion, secrets, logging, and privacy must be designed into every system rather than added after implementation.

### Law 12 — Production Before Expansion

Core workflows must work end to end before new capability is layered on top.

New features must not fragment, duplicate, or destabilize completed systems.

## 2.15 Design Principles

Every interface must follow these principles:

### Decision First

Show the current decision, major risks, strongest strategy, key numbers, and next action before secondary detail.

### Evidence Always Available

Material claims must provide a path to their source, classification, date, and confidence.

### Progressive Disclosure

Show essential information first and reveal advanced detail when requested.

### Context Over Menus

Actions and education should appear where the user needs them instead of forcing unnecessary navigation.

### Fewer Practical Steps

Minimize duplicate entry, unnecessary screens, and avoidable confirmation steps without removing meaningful safeguards.

### Consistent Behavior

The same action, status, classification, and financial term must mean the same thing across web, iPhone, iPad, reports, and exports.

### Outdoor and Field Readability

Mobile views must use readable type, strong contrast, large touch targets, and clear status communication.

### Explain Before Warning

Warnings should state what is wrong, why it matters, and how to resolve or verify it.

### Calm, Premium, and Trustworthy

The product should feel professional and modern without visual noise, gimmicks, or excessive animation.

## 2.16 Non-Negotiable Production Standard

A capability is not complete because a page renders or a function compiles.

It is complete only when a real user can:

1. Enter or import the required information.
2. Complete the intended workflow.
3. Understand the result.
4. Inspect the supporting evidence.
5. Save the outcome.
6. Reopen it later.
7. Recover from expected errors.
8. Use the same authoritative result across supported clients.

BRIX must never present:

- fake production data
- mock production records
- fake analysis
- hardcoded recommendations
- dead navigation
- nonfunctional buttons
- placeholder reports
- simulated success states
- generic AI output disconnected from the deal
- unfinished capability represented as complete
