# BRIX Deal Outcome Intelligence

## Purpose

BRIX is the operating system for real estate deal decisions and outcome learning.

The product should help an investor move from a property signal to a durable decision record:

1. Find the address.
2. Build the deal file.
3. Prepare the deal with notes, tasks, documents, and contacts.
4. Analyze the deal economics.
5. Analyze the contract and terms.
6. Pursue, pass, win, or lose.
7. Move closed assets into PortfolioIQ.
8. Learn which deal characteristics produce better outcomes.

## Product Rule

BRIX is not a listing website and not a generic CRM.

BRIX exists to improve acquisition judgment. Every property should become a structured deal file with facts, assumptions, communications, tasks, analysis, contract risk, offer history, outcome, and lessons learned.

## Module Responsibilities

- FindIQ: find the address and decide whether it deserves investigation.
- DealIQ: analyze the economics, risks, scenarios, and strategy fit.
- ContractIQ: analyze contract terms, obligations, leverage, and transaction risk.
- OfferIQ: turn the recommendation into offer strategy, communications, and negotiation work.
- PipelineIQ: track where the deal stands, who owns the next action, deadlines, and whether it was won, lost, passed, or closed.
- PortfolioIQ: track owned asset performance and preserve outcome intelligence from won, lost, and passed deals.

## Outcome Memory

Win/loss learning is a core system feature.

BRIX should preserve:

- Source
- Market
- Lead path
- Property type
- Strategy
- Asking price
- Offer price
- Seller or agent signals
- Contact history
- Key objections
- Counteroffer terms
- Financing terms
- Insurance concerns
- Tax load
- Rent support
- Repair scope confidence
- Appraisal or valuation confidence
- Contract issues
- Verification gaps
- Final outcome
- Reason the deal won, lost, passed, or closed
- Lesson learned

Only collect information that improves decision quality, confidence, auditability, or future pattern recognition.

## Final Rule

The app should feel like one continuous deal operating system, not disconnected modules.

The user should always know:

- Where do I start?
- What deal am I working?
- What is missing?
- What should I do next?
- Did this deal win or lose?
- What did BRIX learn from it?
