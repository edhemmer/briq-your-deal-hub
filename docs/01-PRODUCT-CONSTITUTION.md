# BRIX Real Estate — Product Constitution

## 1. Product identity

BRIX Real Estate is a Property Deal Relationship Management platform and real estate investment operating system. It organizes the complete relationship between an investor and a property opportunity inside one durable Deal workspace.

BRIX must help an investor answer:

- What is this property?
- What is known, estimated, assumed, inferred, conflicting, or missing?
- Which strategies are viable?
- Which strategy is strongest and why?
- What are the expected returns, capital needs, risks, and constraints?
- What should be verified before acting?
- What should the investor do next?
- How did the Deal change as new evidence arrived?

## 2. Users

BRIX must serve both:

- A beginner investor who needs explanations, guided steps, definitions, warnings, and confidence-building education.
- An experienced residential or commercial investor who needs speed, density, traceability, scenario comparison, exports, and professional-grade controls.

Guided and professional experiences must use the same canonical data and calculations.

## 6. Core product principles

### Decision first

The active Deal must clearly show the current recommendation, strongest strategy, key financial outputs, material risks, missing decision-changing information, deadlines, and next action.

### Evidence before assertion

Every material fact, estimate, assumption, finding, and recommendation must retain source, date, classification, confidence, and relationship to the Deal.

### Continuous underwriting

New financing, photos, voice notes, contracts, governance documents, inspections, appraisals, contractor bids, market information, or user assumptions may change the Deal. BRIX must preserve the prior state and produce a new versioned result.

### Explainability

Every score, recommendation, warning, and maximum offer must explain its inputs, assumptions, evidence, formula version, binding constraint, and missing information.

### Investor control

BRIX recommends. The investor decides. User decisions, overrides, and rationale must be preserved separately from system recommendations.

### Professional boundaries

BRIX may organize and explain legal, tax, insurance, lending, inspection, appraisal, construction, and market information. It must not falsely claim to replace licensed professional advice or professional judgment.

## 7. Required end-to-end Deal lifecycle

BRIX must support:

1. Discover or enter opportunity
2. Create or match Property
3. Create Deal
4. Select intended strategy
5. Import listing and public data
6. Build preliminary assumptions
7. Run underwriting
8. Compare strategies
9. Research market, governance, and financing
10. Plan route and visit property
11. Capture photos, videos, and voice notes
12. Generate visit findings and verification items
13. Structure offer
14. Analyze contract and deadlines
15. Add financing terms
16. Add inspection report
17. Add appraisal
18. Re-underwrite and re-rank
19. Produce reports and comparisons
20. Record decision
21. Close, own, operate, refinance, sell, pass, or archive

Every stage must save, reopen, preserve history, and connect to tasks, deadlines, evidence, contacts, and reports.

## 8. Product quality standard

BRIX must feel like a premium professional operating system:

- Fast
- Calm
- Clear
- Trustworthy
- Modern
- Field-ready
- Dense when useful
- Understandable without being simplistic
- Visually polished without being decorative
- Consistent across web, iPhone, iPad, reports, and shared views

The user must never encounter:

- Dead buttons
- Disconnected modules
- Contradictory values
- Unlabeled stale results
- Silent background failures
- Lost drafts
- Fake analysis
- Placeholder success states
- Unrecoverable uploads
- Generic endless spinners
- Navigation that loses the active Deal
- Reports that disagree with the live Deal

## 9. Product release definition

BRIX is ready for release only when a real investor can create a Deal, complete underwriting, compare strategies, save and reopen, visit a property, capture field evidence, analyze documents, update financing, add inspection and appraisal information, receive a revised recommendation, produce a report, and make a documented decision without developer intervention.
